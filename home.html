10/2 What is Lightning Network?


Here's an option for audio learner. Turn on CC for english subtitle.

I recently attended a blockchain event where the Lightning Network was discussed. As someone unfamiliar with this technology, I decided to do some research. I hope you find it helpful!

Bitcoin, since its inception, has revolutionized the digital world, paving the way for a plethora of decentralized and peer-to-peer digital currencies and technological solutions. However, as Bitcoin expanded, it faced scalability issues, including limited transaction throughput and high fees at times. The Lightning Network was developed as a solution to these problems.

The Lightning Network is an off-chain solution built on the Bitcoin blockchain. It functions much like an alternative road that runs parallel to the main highway (Bitcoin blockchain), facilitating quick, small transactions without causing congestion on the main route. This is achieved through the use of payment channels that allow two parties to complete several transactions off-chain before settling the final balance on the main blockchain.

These payment channels are bidirectional, allowing both parties to send and receive payments. A channel can be opened by two parties depositing BTC in a 2-of-2 multi-sig address. Once the transaction is confirmed, the channel opens, allowing for any number of transactions at a significantly low cost and speed. The channel can be closed when the parties transact through another network chain transaction reflecting their net balances’ changes.

The Lightning Network also has a routing system that facilitates payments between people who do not have a payment channel and uses smart contracts to secure such off-chain transactions. This makes Bitcoin more useful by addressing some of its critical issues, such as scalability, high transaction fees, and transaction speed.

However, like any other system, the Lightning Network is not without its risks. Several security risks have been identified over its history, including the Zombie Attack, the Mass Exit Attack, and the Time-Dilation Attack, among others. But with the implementation of necessary mitigation strategies, the Lightning Network can grow to be a secure and scalable solution for Bitcoin transactions.
